Thank you for buying Bitmoji Lab! You just grabbed a zero-install, zero-account, browser-local creator tool for the BBC micro:bit V2 + NeoPixel LED matrix. Everything runs on your machine — no cloud, no telemetry, no nonsense. This page walks you through the first three things to do.

First 3 Things To Do

1
Wire the matrix + flash the receiver. Hook your NeoPixel matrix to VCC→3V/ext, GND→GND, DIN→P0. Open makecode.microbit.org, paste makecode.ts, Download, drag the .hex onto the board.
2
Open the app. Double-click index.html in Chrome or Edge. Click Connect, pick BBC micro:bit CMSIS-DAP, watch the status dot turn green.
3
Paint, glow, share. Pick an emoji from the library or open Magic Paintbrush. Click-drag to paint, Shift-click to erase. Hit 🚀 SEND TO MICRO:BIT and your matrix lights up. Save your art in a named slot.

Requirements

✓BBC micro:bit V2 (V1 works but is slower on 256 LEDs)
✓NeoPixel / WS2812B LED matrix — 8×8 (64) or 16×16 (256)
✓External power pack (battery or USB power bank) for 64+ LEDs
✓Chrome 90+ or Edge 90+ on desktop (Web Serial)
✓USB data cable (many charge-only cables will not work)
